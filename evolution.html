Evolution

How might we adapt a region and its people with a rich history to a deteriorating environment.

Intro

Hami is a city in Xinjiang landlocked by mountains in Northwest China and thousands of miles away from oceans. In order to deal with the dry climate, early settlers invented Karez, an ancient regional underground water system utilizing underground aquifers which was quite abundant thanks to melting snow water from mountains and groundwater infiltration. Due to reckless region modernization, recent years have witnessed underground water depletion and ecological degradation. Thus, Karez’s current function was useless and was abandoned.

This design focus on how to 're-vein' the Karez system where traditions are preserved, but new functions are brought in so that the new water network will evolve towards a more water abundant, robust, and resilient system, stimulating civilization prosperity, regionalism preservation, and Xinjiang development.

Local methods and technology products will work together and serve as extra water inputs for the Karez system. A more integrated ecology system will be designed that better harvests and integrate local resources. The booming cotton and cloth industry will also provide stages for local arts and traditions.

design background

Segregation between human and nature

Cotton was one of the most important agricultural outputs in Hami, Xinjiang. As production of cotton increased, handicrafts made of woven cloth followed. Agriculture, handicrafts, and cultural traditions were nurtured.

However, due to reckless region modernization,  underground water was depleted and Karez was jeopardized. Water was becoming scarce. The shortage of water had also resulted in the loss of artists and tradition, the community abandoned, agriculture unsustainable, district decline, and the fall of civilization.

Pain points

Transitions of water strategy: from harmony to water shortage

The system of Karez is designed and such system is the cradle of the Xinjiang civilization. But in recent years, this harmony with nature has been disturbed by the modern industry.

design system

A Karez evolution and sustainable workflow integration

Existing Karez infrastructure will be reclaimed and outfitted with new installations. Also, a zoning plan with creating water exchanging zones around the outpours of the Karez to filter agricultural runoff with native plants is designed.

design strategies

Masterplan

Soil is improved, and water shortage eased. The dry land once again becomes thriving with sustainable farming, which can be beneficial and lasting. In the future, Hami could export its unique culture through its art and design, much like it once did in ancient times. Revival and popularity of civilization would lead to tourism, creating a resilient local economy backed by a fully sustainable industry structure.

Phasing

A design of resilient web system in steps

A design timetable was set, so that steps will be taken gradually, and positive environmental effects will also come within years.
